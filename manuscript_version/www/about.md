
Welcome to the **UCSF Tuberculosis Human Dose Prediction Tool** developed by the Savic Lab in support of the **Multi-model Landscape to 
Integrate Machine Learning, Site of Action, and Mechanistic PKPD Translational Tools for Improved Selection of TB Drug Combination Regimens 
and Informed Dose Selection** project funded by the Gates Foundation. This tool predicts clinical efficacious dose ranges for 
anti-TB compounds in development based on *in vitro* and early *in vivo* PK and PD data. 


Key features of this app:

1. **Clinical PK projection:**
Users may select preferred scaling approach(es) to project human PK parameters from preclinical data.

2. **Exposure simulations across biological matrices:**
Drug time-concentration profiles are outputted in plasma, cellular lesions, and caseum, depending on the stage of development.

3. **Target-based coverage assessment:**
PK-PD target attainment is assessed using biologically-relevant PD metrics against PK matrices.

4. **Early bactericidal activity (EBA):** 
Bacterial kill dynamics are simulated to predict early clinical efficacy as drug monotherapy or in combination with established backbones.

Additional information on the modeling framework can be found
[here](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37321622/).
